Netflix Trends and Insights Dashboard

Netflix has released 3.735 of Movies and 1.955 of TV Shows from 1945-2022 (67 years), with the number of movies almost twice the number of TV Shows. US, India and Japan are the countries that produce the most titles. For the last decade, the highest release of Movies title is in 2019 with 540 of titles and 306 of TV Shows in 2020. There is an increase in film released and it peaked in 2019, then there was a sharp decline start in 2022. There are 11 of age certification. While, the most titles are in TV-MA rating, followed by R rating. It means that the customer segment that suitable for Netflix is for people above 17 years old. The most titles are between 90-120 minutes in duration with 1.823 of titles, followed by titles in between 30-60 minutes duration. The highest number of actors who participated in titles was in 2019 with 8.892 actors. The highest number of directors who participated in titles was in 2019 with 623 actors. While Raul Campos become the director who directed the most films with 21 titles. The IMDb score of the titles vary from minimum in 1.5 and the highest in 9.6.

Dominant Aircraft Analysis Dashboard

The map highlights regional flight activities, showcasing airline dominance based on passenger traffic. Lufthansa, Eurowings, and Swissair emerge as the top airlines with high passenger traffic. A bar chart categorizes aircraft types (e.g., A320, B738) based on flight frequency, with the A320 model leading. This dashboard helps users analyze aviation trends by country, airline, and aircraft type while contextualizing them with COVID-19 data.

Titanic Analysis Dashboard

Passenger Overview: Average Age: 30.15 years, with males averaging slightly higher (30.72) than females (29.15). Passenger Count: 981, showcasing the diverse population onboard. Fare Analysis: The average ticket fare was $37.43, with a maximum fare of $76. Survival Analysis: By Class: First-class passengers had the highest survival rate, with fewer survivors in third class. By Age Groups: Younger passengers, particularly those under 20, had higher survival probabilities. By Gender: Females had a notably higher survival rate compared to males. Passenger Distribution: Class: Third-class passengers formed the majority (46.48%), followed by second (27.22%) and first class (26.3%). Embarkation Points: Most passengers boarded from Southampton, followed by Cherbourg and Queenstown. Global Representation: A geographical map illustrates passengers' home destinations, highlighting North America, Europe, and parts of Asia as significant sources. Additional Insights: Survival rates varied across cabins, with cabin "C" having the highest survival count. The dashboard highlights key insights on gender distribution and survival based on cabin and embarkation points.

Hybrid Learning Environment Dashboard

The dashboard reflects feedback from 69 respondents across 10 countries. Key metrics include Learning Duration: Majority of students engaged in hybrid learning for 6 months to a year. Satisfaction Levels: 34 respondents reported being satisfied, while a smaller percentage expressed dissatisfaction. Demographics: Respondents primarily come from Europe and Africa, with an age range mostly between 25-34. Field of Study: Business & Management and Engineering are the most represented disciplines. This dashboard offers valuable insights into hybrid learning, facilitating actionable decisions to enhance the educational experience.
